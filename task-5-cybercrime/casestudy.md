## Case Study: The "Urgent Fee" UPI Fraud

**What the Crime Is:**
This is a case of UPI (Unified Payments Interface) Fraud, specifically an overpayment or refund scam where the attacker manipulates the victim into authorizing a payment under false pretenses.

**How It Happens (Step-by-Step):**
1. The scammer targets a victim who recently listed an item for sale on an online marketplace.
2. The scammer agrees to buy the item immediately and sends a fake screenshot showing they accidentally transferred ₹5,000 instead of the agreed ₹500.
3. The scammer acts panicked and urgently requests the victim to refund the ₹4,500 difference via UPI.
4. The scammer sends a "QR code to receive the refund." 
5. When the victim scans the code and enters their UPI PIN—believing they are returning the extra funds—money is actually deducted from their own account.

**Who is Usually Targeted:**
College students and young adults are frequently targeted because they heavily rely on digital payment apps for fast transactions but might not fully realize that a UPI PIN is only used to send money, never to receive it.

**The Consequences:**
The victim suffers immediate financial loss. Because UPI transactions transfer instantly between bank accounts, reversing the transaction and recovering the stolen funds is extremely difficult. The student also experiences significant stress and a loss of trust in digital peer-to-peer marketplaces.
